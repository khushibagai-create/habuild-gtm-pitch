The narrative

Ask Saurabh.

"Poochho Saurabh se"

Each week has a theme. Members ask Saurabh their question in the app. He answers some live in class, names them, and shows the feature that helps. The app is where they ask, and where they get the answer.

One GTM pitch · theme by theme · starts Monday 6 July
Why this

Members open the app for a reason, not for an ad.

We already have the features. What we are missing is a reason to open the app that feels personal. A question for Saurabh is that reason. He is why members are here in the first place, so we build on that.

How it works

Ask, answer, open.

1

Theme opens

In class and on WhatsApp: "This week is about breathing. Ask Saurabh your question in the app."

2

Members ask

They open the app and send their question, using the feedback card. A chip for the struggle, plus a short line or a voice note.

3

Saurabh answers

We pick the questions that fit the feature we want to show. In class: "Sunita, who has completed 400 yoga days, asked me this." He answers, and shows the feature.

4

The member comes back

"Saurabh answered your question today. Watch it, and here is the feature he showed." They open the app.

Inside the app

We reuse the feedback Story template.

The "Ask Saurabh" card is the feedback Story template we already built: an info pill, a heading, one chip question, and a short text box. Nothing new to design. The comms team can publish it like any other Story, and change the theme each week.

  • Same template, new content. Info pill carries the theme, the chip captures the common struggle, the text box holds the real question.
  • Easy to answer. A voice note option means members who do not like typing can still ask.
  • One inbox for the team. The questions come into a simple list, and we pick the ones that fit this week's feature.
  • Changes every week. New theme, new pill, same card.
Why it works

It runs on the bond members already have with Saurabh.

Saurabh is the pull

For a 40+ member, hearing Saurabh take her name, mention her milestone like 400 yoga days, and answer her question in class is a big moment. It celebrates her journey too. Nothing in an app can match that.

The feature is honest

The app shows up as the answer to a real question, not as an ad. So the pitch lands softly.

Asking is an app action

To ask, you open the app. That is the reason to install that we have been missing.

The answers become content

Each answer is a short clip for WhatsApp and for the app's Status. One activity feeds the Community Events feature too.

A worked example

Breathing week, end to end.

Members ask (in the app)

"I feel breathless climbing stairs." · "My mind keeps running at night, I can't sleep." · "Is there a right way to breathe?"

We pick the ones that fit Box Breathing

The sleep and stress questions connect cleanly to box breathing, so we hand those to Saurabh for the class.

Saurabh answers in class

"Sunita, who has completed 400 yoga days, asked me, my mind keeps running at night. Sunita, this is exactly what box breathing is for. And in the app you can do it at your own pace, your own count, every night."

The follow-up

Sunita gets a message: "Saurabh answered your question today. Watch it, and open Box Breathing." Others get the clip on WhatsApp and in Status.

What we need to decide and build

A few calls, then we start.

To decide

  • Where members ask. In the app only (so asking is the reason to install), or on WhatsApp too. I lean app only for the themed weeks.
  • How wide. One batch first (say one 21-day group) or the whole base.
  • Naming members in class (first name and city).

To build

  • The Ask Saurabh card reuses the feedback Story template. Small build.
  • A simple inbox for the team to read questions and pick a few each day.
  • The Hydration feature for week 1 is still being built.
  • The answer clip goes to WhatsApp and Status.
