Habuild · App GTM One good habit a week
The narrative

One good habit. Every week.

"Har hafte, ek achhi aadat"

Habuild means habit building. So every week we build one small good habit together, starting with water. Members ask Saurabh their question about that week's habit in the app. He answers some live in class, names them, and shows the feature that makes the habit easy to keep.

One GTM pitch · one habit a week · starts Monday 6 July
The opening, in Saurabh's words

"Habuild ka matlab hai habit building."

"A lot of members ask me, what does Habuild mean? Habuild means habit building. That is all we do. We want to build the habit of moving your body, every day. And from this week, every week we will pick one small thing and build that habit with you too. We start with water. Who does not want to drink enough water? So this week, ask me anything about water and hydration, and I will answer you in class."

The idea

Each week is one habit. The app is where you keep it.

Every feature we have built is really one good habit: drink water, breathe to calm down, care for the spot that hurts, eat one healthy meal, log your check, keep your mind sharp, take a walk. We introduce one a week. By the end, a member's whole day is a chain of small good habits, and every one of them lives in the app. That is what No-Zero Days already rewards.

What we are doing

Three parts to this GTM.

The weekly habit is the heart of it. Two more pieces support it, so members can find their way around the app and understand why it is worth opening.

PART 1

One feature a week, as one good habit

Each week we introduce one feature as one good habit. Saurabh opens it in class, members ask their question in the app, and the feature is what keeps the habit going. Seven habits across the campaign.

  • How to update the app
  • A short tutorial for every feature

Saurabh points to it in class and on WhatsApp whenever someone gets stuck.

PART 3

An onboarding video from Saurabh

We need one video from Saurabh where he talks about why the app matters, the value it adds beyond the live class. New and existing members see it when they first open the app, so the first thing they hear is from him.

How a week runs

Habit, ask, answer, keep it.

1

The habit opens

In class and on WhatsApp: "This week we build the water habit. Ask me anything about hydration, in the app."

2

Members ask

They open the app and send their question, using the feedback card. A chip for the struggle, plus a short line or a voice note.

3

Saurabh answers

We pick the questions that fit the feature we want to show. In class: "Sunita, who has completed 400 yoga days, asked me this." He answers, and shows the feature that helps keep the habit.

4

The habit sticks

"Saurabh answered your question today. Watch it, and here is the tracker to keep your water habit going." They open the app, and keep the habit there.

The "Ask Saurabh" card is the feedback Story template we already built: an info pill, a heading, one chip question, and a short text box. Nothing new to design. The comms team can publish it like any other Story, and change the theme each week.

Why it works

It runs on the bond members already have with Saurabh.

Saurabh is the pull

For a 40+ member, hearing Saurabh take her name, mention her milestone like 400 yoga days, and answer her question in class is a big moment. It celebrates her too. Nothing in an app can match that.

The feature is honest

The app shows up as the answer to a real question, not as an ad. So the pitch lands softly.

Asking is an app action

To ask, you open the app. That is the reason to install that we have been missing.

The answers become content

Each answer is a short clip for WhatsApp and for the app's Status. One activity feeds the Community Events feature too.

A worked example

Week 1, the water habit, end to end.

Saurabh opens the habit in class

"Habuild means habit building. This week we build one small habit together: water. Who does not want to drink enough water? So ask me anything about water in the app, and I will answer you here in class."

Members ask (in the app)

"How much water should I drink in a day?" · "I just forget to drink." · "Should it be warm or cold?"

We pick the ones that fit the water tracker

The "I just forget" question connects cleanly to the tracker, so we hand that one to Saurabh for the class.

Saurabh answers in class

"Sunita, who has completed 400 yoga days, asked me, I just forget to drink water. Sunita, this is exactly why we added the water tracker in the app. It reminds you, and you tick each glass. Let us build this habit together this week."

The habit sticks

Sunita gets a message: "Saurabh answered your question today. Watch it, and open the water tracker." Others get the clip on WhatsApp and in Status.
