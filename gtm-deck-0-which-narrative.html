The decision

How do we get members to use the app?

A plan to give members a reason to open the Habuild app every day. And the one choice we need to make first.

July to August 2026 · Starts in class, Monday 5 July
The problem

Members love Habuild.
They just don't open the app.

For most paid members, Habuild is the 6:30 AM class and the WhatsApp group. The app is there, but they never really use it.

the goal: 80% install, and 50% open the app at least 4 times a month
The idea

We're not just adding features.
We want members to see Habuild as part of their whole day.

Today, Habuild means one morning class. We want it to mean more: breathing, food, walks, looking after your body, your progress, your community. Each feature is one more reason to open the app. We share it where members already hear from us: in class, and on WhatsApp.

The one choice

One idea, two ways to say it. Which one do we use?

Both do the same job. They just say it differently, and lean on the class more or less. Open either one to see the full plan.

Narrative A

The class is just the beginning

"Class to sirf shuruaat hai"

Every feature is the next thing after class. Saurabh teaches box breathing in class, then tells members to finish it on the app. It feels natural, because he is already doing it in front of them.

Line he repeats
"Class poori hui? Asli din ab shuru hota hai."

Narrative B

Your whole day, in the app

"Aapka poora din, ab Habuild app par"

Habuild is with members all day: their breathing, their meals, their evening walk, their knee, their mind, their progress, their community. Everything in one place.

Line he repeats
"Subah se raat tak, Habuild aapke saath."
See Narrative B in full
Use A if we want the class to do most of the work, and a strong start with box breathing. It is the easiest thing for Saurabh to say. The promise is smaller.
Use B if we want the bigger "everything in one app" idea. It makes the Today tab the main thing. The link to the class is weaker.
